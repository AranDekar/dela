About us

Dela Ensemble.

“Dela Ensemble” is a classical group founded in early 2015 by Aran Dekar(Tar), Sina Arbabzadeh (Santour), Nadia Dardashti (Vocalist), and Mehdi Mansouri ( Setar & Daf). By 2016 Ali Tahamtani (Tar Bass), Amir Ghadiri (Violin & Tonbak), Ashkan Irani (Tonbak) joined the group, finally Danial Bozorgi (Vocalist) completed this group by 2017. The name “Dela” was inspired by it’s meaning in the hope that, what comes from the hearts of it’s members, reaches the hearts of it’s audiences, through generations of maestros of Sonati music of Iran and the poetry of legends such as Rumi, Forough Farokhzad and Sohrab Sepehri, and vocalists such as Ghamar, Mohammad Reza Shajarian and Alireza Ghorbani. Dela Ensemble members have had performances with Maestro Saket in Sydney Australia and continue creating and composing original pieces by Aran Dekar and Mehdi Mansouri for future performances.

Ali Tahamtan.

Ali was born in 1981 in a family whose mother was an Iranian classical music singer. He Started His music from the age of 10 performing with a group of other kids in a local TV channel in Iran playing keyboard and piano. Later on at the age of 15 his passion in music moved toward Iranian music and started playing Tar, one of the most famous traditional instruments in Iran since then. Ali had the opportunity to learn from famous masters such as Rahim Fathali zadeh, Navid Afghah and Keivan Saket who is prominent for Technique and music knowledge. Ali has performed in several national and international festivals and alongside with other members of DELA ensemble desire to represent Iranian music culture to interact with other International audiences and music trends.

Ashkan Ghaffari.

Born in 1983 in Tehran Iran, Ashkan Ghaffari’ s music talent first discovered while he was 5. He showed a lot of interest in rhythm and music especially a traditional Persian instrument called Tombak also called Zarb where he started his training with Mastro Helmi. He also had the opportunity to be the student of Siamak banaie, one of the best teachers trained by mastro Mohammad Esmaïli. To date, training the traditional Tombak lessons to the younger generation, beside his professional career, he managed to perform in some Iranians and Australian festivals i.e. Sydney’s harmony day.

Aran Dekar.

Aran is a contemporary musician and composer, he has been playing the Tar and the Setar, two of the most famous traditional instruments in Iran for more than 20 years now, he started his journey in music in 1997, by learning the Radif from his tutor Master “Ataa Jangook” one of the greatest Tar instrumentalists in Iran, he started learning the techniques of the Tar from his Master Mr Keivan Saket (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keivan_Saket) . He involved in festivals such as Bellingen and Harmony Day festival. He managed a concert in Sydney in 2015 performing along with his maestro Mr Keivan Saket in the ensemble. His plan along with other artists in the “Dela” ensemble is to compose new contemporary music parts and perform great concerts in Australia.

Mehdi Mansouri.

Born 1980, Tehran Iran. Started Setar classes with Mastero Davood Azad in 1996 and contained learning from other great musicians till today. During these years started self learning Daf and Tanbour and completed several courses in Daf with Amin Dadvari, Yavar Ahmadi far and Tanbour with Arash Shahriari and Sadegh Mahdavi. Still attending Setar and Vocal master classes with Master Sadegh Mahdavi. Before Moving to Australia had several performances as Setar player and back vocalist with Sarv Ensemble in Iran. Album of these concerts is going to be published shortly.

Sina Arbabzadeh.

Sina is a Santur instrumentalist and one of Dela Ensemble’s members and co-founders in Sydney, Australia. Born in 1981 in Shiraz, Iran, he became interested in music from early ages and began learning Santur at the age of 9 by attending Master Babaei’s Santur lessons in Isfahan where he grew his talent for music until age of 19. He studied Radif at the same time with his tutor and accomplished Maestro Abolhasan Saba’s Radif successfully. He moved to Australia in 2011 and along his professional career as a Civil Engineer and his another passion for photography, he strived to continue his music by collaborating with other musicians locally and internationally. His turning point as a musician was when he performed along Maestro Keivan Saket (One of the greatest Tar instrumentalist and composers in Iran) as part of Saket Ensembel Concert in Sydney, 2014. He aims to develop his technics in playing Santur (i.e. his main instrument) and Tanboor (i.e. his secondary instrument), and also learn and practice composing technics.

Nadia Dardashti.

was born in Tehran, Iran into a musically spirited family. Growing up the sound of maestro Younes Dardashti her second cousin, filled the air in awe of her youthful soul. Leaving her beloved country in despair at a young age after the revolution, left her with the yearning to pursue her passion for singing. The passion that remained unleashed until many years later, initially in USA and later on in Sydney, Australia where she resides presently. Nadia has studied the fundamentals of Persian classical music from Master composer Siamak Farokhsereshti at Madakto art centre, and Master Sadegh Mahdavi. Nadia has had performances in USA and Australia, and has been the Vocalist for Dela Ensemble since 2015.

Danial Bozorgi.

Born in 1982, as a Persian classic music vocalist, Danial Bozorgi's musical gift was first nurtured and instructed in a family of talented musicians mostly trained by Mastro Ali Tajvidi. He has been a student of Davood Fayazi, one of the prominent vocalists and teachers trained by Mastro Ismail Mehrtash. To date, inspired by his deep interest in Persian classic music, besides his professional career, he has managed to have performances in a number of international and national festivals and concerts in Iran, Malaysia and Australia. He has also been one of the founding members of Mastoor ensemble in Western Australia.

Amir Ghadiri.

Amir Ghadiri (born in 1981 in Tehran, Iran) is an Iranian musician from a musically talented family. His musical journey anticipated when he was 9. His father, an Iranian Santoor player and traditional vocalist, encouraged him to begin studying musical instruments. Amir formally followed his passion for Tombak, a goblet drum from Persia, under the supervision of the legendary Hojat Alborzi. Amir’s talent in learning Tombak and rhythm helped him achieve the professional level at the very young age of 16, enabling him to have his first stage performance at the “Vesal Theatre” as part of the “Nakisa” Ensemble. Amir began to show interest in learning Iranian “Dastgah”s and also string instrument violin in 1995, attending music lessons of Master Davood Ganjei and later on Mohammad Moghaddasi. A brief list of his official stage performances as either a violinist or Tombak player are as followed: - Vesal Theatre - 1997 - Nakisa Ensemble - Andisheh Theatre - 2000 - Nakisa Ensemble - IRIB Theatre - 2001 - Nakisa Ensemble - Amir Kabir Association of Arts - 2011 - Ghazal Ensemble - Marist 180 Persian New Year Celebration - 2017 - Dela Ensemble Amir has always had the passion of passing his knowledge and experience in music to the younger generation. Amir joined Dela Ensemble, a young Iranian band, in 2015 and still attends the rehearsals for their future public performances.

AmirSalar Makhzani.

Born in Tehran in 1986, Studied telecommunications engineering in Malaysia Started playing Daf at the age of 11 under influence of Nematollahi Dervishes gatherings and learned Daf Maqams from them. then joined Mr.Keyvan Pahlevan classes for both Daf and Tonbak , also attended Yousef Ashrafi’s classes in principles of rhythm. He has played in Multiple festivals including AUS Global day 2006,2007 as a part of Sheida ensemble and also played at Sama ensemble in multiple performances. He moved to Australia since 2014 and have played with Davood Ardalan on his Azeri tour, with Madakto in Azizi Shahrokh tour and now proudly joined Dela ensemble.

Events

East Meets West Concert. April 2014

This concert was a persian classical music along with choreography held in UNSW university in clancy auditorium, there were around 600 audiences.

Cellebration for new Persian Year. June 2017.

Dela was delighted to participate in a love sharing and multiculturalism event and performed for MARIST 180 ( non profit organisation) To celebrate Persian new year with some other people from various backgrounds and presented some Persian classical music.

Shab-e-yalda (Yalda night). December 2017.

Shab-e-yalda is the longest night in north hemisphere and Iranians celebrate this night as it is opening to a new season which is spring, Dela performed a classical music there for audiences.

Grand Charity Concert April 2018.

Iranian House of Music conducted a collaborative Charity Concert to support the survivors of the recent earthquake in Kermanshah/Kurdistan, IRAN. This event featured five well-known Iranian ensembles and orchestras playing a diverse genre of repertoire made up of variety of beautiful melodies and songs. All the donations joined the funds collected from around Australia to invest in an infrastructural development project that yielded public benefits in a poorly resourced region in Kermanshah district. This event was carried out through a collaboration with “OASIS Charity” and the “Support for Iranian survivors (SUFIS)” campaign.
